HELP AND FAQs

Still have questions?
Contact us at 1-800-960-2184 or Email us at support@midigator.com

Is my information safe?
Midigator uses the latest security technologies and releases security improvements frequently. We also sign a mutual non-disclosure agreement with every client.
Can multiple users be logged in at the same time with the same login credentials?
Yes! You can use the same login credentials on different computers at the same time. You can also contact customer relations to add additional users to your company's account for free.
Can Midigator integrate with multiple CRMs?
Yes, just contact customer relations when you add an additional CRM and we will integrate through API to grab all the necessary order information.
How do I add an account or MID?
Simply go to the MIDs section and click Add Merchant Account. Add the full MID number without leading zeros, full domain or URL, the CRM containing this MID, Gateway IDs from the CRM, merchant portal login URL and the username/password combination (case sensitive!).
What happens when a MID is no longer active?
Go to the MIDs section, click the edit button next to the MID and change its status to inactive. You will still receive chargebacks and other information on inactive MIDs.
What's a Notification?
The notifications section allows you to take action when a MID is in trouble. You can set up an email or text notification when certain parameters are met. For example, you may want to be emailed when the CB count for MID 12345 exceeds 50 chargebacks during the current month. You may also want to be emailed when the CB ratio exceeds 2% for all MIDs during the last 30 days. You can set up as many notifications as you like in order to stay on top of your business.
Can I delete a notification?
Yes, just go to the notifications section to pause, edit or delete the notification altogether.
What's a Threshold?
Thresholds allow you to highlight cells on the "MIDs with Chargebacks" table to visually see when a field has surpassed a certain level. For example, you can highlight the CB count cell, if it exceeds 50 on a certain MID. You can also highlight the CB ratio cell when the ratio exceeds 2%. When cells are highlighted, you can better monitor your MID activity on the table.
How does Midigator calculate transaction counts?
Midigator counts all individual orders from the CRM by gateway ID. This number does not include declines!
What is the $ Ratio?
The dollar ratio is the total dollar amount of chargebacks divided by the total dollar amount of transactions. Some processors use this ratio instead of the chargeback ratio to monitor account performance.
What is the CB Ratio?
The chargeback ratio is the total chargeback count divided by the total transaction count.
How is my Mastercard ratio calculated?
Your MC Ratio is calculated as Current Month Chargebacks / Last Month Transactions unlike other card providers that calculate it as Current Month Chargebacks / Current Month Transactions.
How do I find out why a chargeback was marked as Did Not Fight?
Hover over the Did Not Fight text in the Results column, which can be found under the Completed tab on the Manager page.
What are the definitions of reason codes used for the status Did Not Fight?
